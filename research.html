Research

Berkeley Computational Imaging Lab

From Winter 2018 to Summer 2020, I performed research in the Berkeley Computational Imaging Lab as an undergraduate researcher. I developed an inexpensive lensless camera prototype that can reconstruct images from 90% compression. [source code]

  • Multi-sensor lensless imaging: synthetic large-format sensing with a disjoint sensor array [paper]
  • Multi-sensor DiffuserCam: lensless imaging [poster]
  • Multi-sensor DiffuserCam: methodology summary [course paper]

Carnegie Mellon Institute for Software Research

In Summer 2018, I worked on an explainable AI research project at the Carnegie Mellon Institute for Software Research as part of the NSF REU-SE program. I built a tool for boosting domain-level users' understanding of Markov Decision Planner AI agents.

  • Interactive Explanation for Planning-Based Systems [paper]
  • Interactive Explanation of Robot Navigation Planning [poster1] [poster2]
